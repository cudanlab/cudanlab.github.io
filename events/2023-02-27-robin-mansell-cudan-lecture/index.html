Robin Mansell: Digital Governance: Comparing Myths and Practice

When: 2023-02-27 14:00-16:00 (Tallinn time)
Where: online

The event is public via zoom: https://zoom.us/j/99209544202 Websites: Mansell, R. and Steinmueller, W. E. (2022) ‘DeNaturalising Digital Platforms: Is Mass Individualisation Here to Stay?, International Journal of Communication, 16: 461-481. https://ijoc.org/index.php/ijoc/article/view/13093/3655 home page https://www.lse.ac.uk/media-and-communications/people/academic-staff/robin-mansell

Abstract – The claim that a balancing of commercial, state and citizen interests in digital innovation can be achieved is examined as a myth that works to conceal commitments to digital innovation outcomes that privilege the interests of technology companies and states. This talk emphasises the need to pay attention to the implementation of digital governance interventions if myths are to be exposed and digital systems consistent with citizens’ interests in autonomy, dignity and democracy are to become more prominent.

Additional information: Robin Mansell is a Professor Emerita at the department of Media and Communications, London School of Economics and Political Science.
